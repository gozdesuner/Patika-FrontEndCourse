Biography

Lev Nikolayevich Tolstoy (1828-1910), commonly known as Leo Tolstoy, was a Russian writer, philosopher, and thinker. Born into an aristocratic family, he is best known for his monumental novels, such as War and Peace and Anna Karenina. His works explored themes of morality, family, and human existence. Later in life, Tolstoy embraced a simple lifestyle, focusing on spiritual and ethical writings that influenced generations.

Famous Works

  • War and Peace (1869)
  • Anna Karenina (1877)
  • The Death of Ivan Ilyich (1886)
  • Resurrection (1899)
  • A Confession (1882)
